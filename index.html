Things in Rings

A Venn diagram party game

How to Play

  1. Generate clues. Open the Clue Generator and pick a difficulty. You'll get three clues — one for each ring:
    • Attribute — a physical property (e.g. "Can be eaten")
    • Context — where or how it's found (e.g. "Found in a kitchen")
    • Word — a spelling/phonetic rule (e.g. "Has exactly 5 letters")
  2. Draw words. On the Board, each player has a hand of word cards. Click Draw to refill.
  3. Place words. Drag each word card into the region of the Venn diagram where it belongs:
    • Inside a single ring if it matches only that clue.
    • In the overlap if it matches two or three clues.
    • In the None zone if it matches nothing.
  4. Debate and score. Players take turns challenging each other's placements. Agree on the rules, argue about the edge cases, and have fun.

Tips

  • Hold Shift and drag to reposition the circles and labels on the board.
  • Start on Easy for your first game, then move to Hard for heated debates.
  • The best rounds happen when a word could plausibly go in multiple spots.
